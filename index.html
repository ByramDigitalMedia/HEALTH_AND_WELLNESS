Classic Massage

Classic Massage – One of the most effective methods for treating and preventing many diseases. Classic Massage not only relieves pain, swelling, and adhesions, and normalizes organ and system functions, but also accelerates tissue regeneration, restores muscle tone, improves musculoskeletal performance, and strengthens the joint-ligament system, providing an overall restorative effect on the body.

During a Classic Massage, waste products, toxins, and metabolic byproducts are eliminated, fats are broken down, and the skin becomes smooth, firm, and elastic, reducing the appearance of cellulite. The effects of Classic Massage are especially noticeable when taken in courses 2–3 times a year. It can be a full-body massage (general massage) or target specific areas (localized massage). Depending on the duration and applied pressure, Classic Massage can be either relaxing or toning.

Classic Massage

Indications

Cardiovascular diseases; respiratory system disorders; injuries and diseases of the musculoskeletal system; nervous system disorders and injuries; digestive system diseases outside the acute phase; inflammatory diseases of the male and female reproductive organs in subacute or chronic stages; ear, throat, and nose diseases; metabolic disorders.

Classic Massage

Contraindications

Purulent processes of any localization; infectious, fungal, or unknown etiology skin and subcutaneous tissue diseases; allergies with hemorrhagic or other rashes; angioedema (Quincke’s edema); tuberculosis; sexually transmitted diseases; osteomyelitis; acute febrile conditions and associated inflammatory processes; gallstone and kidney stone disease; blood disorders; lymphadenitis, lymphangitis; acute inflammation; thrombosis; menstruation; pregnancy (especially in the last month when lying on the stomach); significant varicose veins; malignant and benign tumors; acute respiratory infections and within 3–5 days after the acute period; vascular inflammation (angiitis and thromboangiitis); bleeding disorders and tendency to bleed.

Wellness Massage

An indispensable tool originating from traditional medicine, used as a means to help people survive in extreme conditions. The reasons included injuries and trauma associated with daily life at the time, hard physical labor, and wars. And, of course, the main reason that led people to focus on the abdomen was pain. Today, this therapy is known as visceral chiropractic.

Visceral chiropractic involves influencing internal organs through pressing, tapping, shifting, and massage to restore organ position and microcirculation in their surrounding spaces. These actions help normalize many metabolic processes in the body and eliminate functional disorders.

With the help of abdominal wellness massage, common problems such as venous and lymphatic congestion, spasms, and organ prolapse—which are frequent causes of pathological conditions—can be successfully addressed. The proposed method of external impact on the internal organs of the abdomen allows for effective correction of these pathologies. During treatment, the specialist applies controlled pressure to the affected organ at the proper angle and following specific techniques. As a result, organ functions are restored and normalized, which positively affects overall health, promotes longevity, improves work capacity, and enhances psycho-emotional well-being.

Honey Massage

The ability of honey to absorb toxins is truly unique. During the massage, honey deeply penetrates the tissues, absorbing all accumulated "waste." No other therapeutic or cosmetic product can provide such a complete and effective cleansing of the body’s surface. As a result of the procedure, skin turgor and elasticity improve, subcutaneous fat capsules soften, and sometimes even dissolve. The biologically active substances in honey have strong penetrating properties and enhance tissue regeneration.

Before the actual honey massage, a warming muscle massage is performed. Then honey is applied to the warmed body and rubbed in, massaging the body with honey. After a while, the honey is partially absorbed and partially thickens, making normal massage movements impossible. At this point, the technique changes, and the massage is performed using a “sticking and lifting” method, where the therapist’s hands repeatedly adhere to and lift from the patient’s body.
